A Clear Path Through Your Legal Challenges

Reformation Practice and Legal Advocacy Network (RePLAN) is more than just a non-profit—it’s a movement for justice, dignity, and second chances

We believe that people are more than the mistakes they’ve made. That’s why our work focuses on prison reform, legal advocacy, and family support, ensuring that incarcerated individuals and their loved ones are not forgotten or silenced.

Our Mission

To promote sustainable prison reform and reintegration of ex-inmates through legal advocacy, public awareness, stakeholder engagement, and capacity building — guided by international standards and national laws.

Aims & Objectives

Here are our aims and objectives

Advocate for Reintegration and Family Support

We champion policies and programs to support inmates and their families during and after incarceration.

Support Funding for Inmate Families

We work to secure funding through legislation and private sector partnerships to aid inmate families.

Promote Skill-Building in Correctional Centres

We facilitate training programs to equip inmates with valuable skills for reintegration.

Collaborate with Private Sectors and NGOs

We partner with organizations to enhance our impact on prison reform and support.

Use Global Frameworks (UN Rules, NCSA 2019)

We align with international standards like UN Rules and Nigeria's NCSA 2019 to improve prison systems.

Push for Legal Reforms and Better Human Rights

We advocate for updated laws and enhanced human rights standards in the justice system.
